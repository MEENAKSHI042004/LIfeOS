Rule-Based Scoring
Each module uses explicit, auditable formulas. Habit module computes:

score = (completed/total) × 100 × streak_bonus

Every score traces to a specific user action. No black-box inference.
Statistical Insight Layer
Compares today's score against rolling 7-day mean (μ) and standard deviation (σ).

If score < μ − σ → corrective insight fires.
If score > μ + σ → positive reinforcement fires.

Trigger Conditions
Insights fire deterministically on thresholds:

• habit < 70 → "Complete 3 habits before noon"
• finance ≥ 90 → "Exceptional discipline"
• goal < 60 → "Dedicate 30 min to top goal"
• overall > 80 → "Top 10% — maintain streak"
Why this architecture? For a personal productivity OS, explainability matters more than raw ML accuracy. Users need to understand why they got a score to act on it. The rule-based core ensures every insight is auditable, while the statistical anomaly layer adds adaptive personalisation without requiring training data.
